Launching a New Airline from Jaipur

A Data-Driven Market, Forecasting & Network Strategy Project

1. Project Background — Why Jaipur?

When I began this project, one question guided my entire research: “Can Jaipur realistically support a new airline with sustainable international operations?”

India’s aviation market is growing aggressively, yet Jaipur—despite being a major tourism and business hub— remains overshadowed by Delhi.

This gap presented the perfect analytical challenge: Could a new carrier, Great Owl Airlines, capture untapped demand and operate profitably from Jaipur?

2. Market Understanding — Looking Beyond Raw Numbers

My first step was to understand who actually flies from Jaipur and, more importantly, who doesn’t.

I mapped the entire catchment area, analysing:

  • Population distribution
  • Tourism flows
  • Corporate travel demand
  • Income levels and aviation propensity

A recurring pattern emerged — high-value passengers were leaking to Delhi.

This indicated something crucial: The demand existed — but Jaipur’s supply didn’t.

3. Forecasting Demand — Gravity + Time-Series

A single forecasting method is often misleading, so I combined:

  • Gravity Model — structural demand estimation
  • Time-Series Forecasting — trend + stability analysis

The Gravity Model suggested 10,434–72,237 annual passengers.

Time-series showed that adding new international routes stabilised Jaipur’s overall passenger trend.

4. Airline Simulation — Building Great Owl Airlines

Using Airline Online, I simulated Great Owl Airlines with a fleet tailored to Jaipur.

Over two simulated years, the airline expanded to 13 international destinations — with Asian routes outperforming:

  • Dubai
  • Singapore
  • Bangkok
  • Kuala Lumpur

The London route underperformed, highlighting the need for right-sized aircraft and cost control.

5. Financial Performance

  • 2026 Profit: $10.9M — strong load factors + efficient scheduling
  • 2027 Loss: $16M — maintenance mis-timing + high advertising spend

6. Final Recommendation

The airline is viable with the right strategy:

  • Focus on Asian short–medium haul markets
  • Use right-sized aircraft
  • Improve MRO scheduling
  • Targeted digital marketing

Ultimately, Great Owl Airlines has a strong opportunity to become Jaipur’s international gateway to Asia.
